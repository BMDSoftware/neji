About becas

becas is a web application, API and widget for biomedical concept identification. It is an ongoing research project developed by the University of Aveiro Bioinformatics group, with funding from several Portuguese and European projects.

It helps researchers, healthcare professionals and developers in the identification of over 1,200,000 biomedical concepts in text and PubMed abstracts.

As a user, you can learn to use the webapp in the help section. If you want to integrate becas annotation services in your own text-processing pipeline, read more about our API. Web developers can augment websites with text annotation and highlighting capabilities using the becas widget.

Features

becas provides annotations for isolated, nested and intersected entities. It identifies concepts from multiple semantic groups, providing preferred names and enriching them with references to public knowledge resources. You can choose the types of entities you want to identify and highlight or mute specific entities in real-time.

To facilitate annotation of PubMed abstracts, becas automatically fetches publications from NCBI servers and renders them with identified concepts highlighted.

Recognized concepts

becas uses dictionary-matching techniques to recognize species, anatomical concepts, microRNAs, enzymes, chemicals, drugs, diseases, metabolic pathways, cellular components, biological processes and molecular functions. For this, we compiled a database of concepts from multiple meta-sources, including UMLS, NCBI BioSystems, LexEBI, ChEBI, miRBase and the Gene Ontology. Genes and proteins are identified using machine-learning techniques with normalization, built over Gimli.

Validation

becas was validated against CRAFT, AnEM and the NCBI Diseases corpora, achieving f-measure results for overlap matching of 76% for genes and proteins, 95% for species, 65% for chemicals, 83% for cellular components, 92% for cells, 63% for molecular functions and biological processes, 83% for anatomical entities, and 85% for diseases. These results are on par with current state-of-the-art biomedical annotation systems.
